## Design Context

### Users
Iruka is used by DeFi operators, protocol analysts, and agent builders who need a serious monitoring surface for defining, reviewing, and acting on blockchain signals. They use it as an operating console, not as a marketing-first product. The signed-in app should feel calm, exact, and trustworthy while still carrying a distinctive Iruka identity.

### Brand Personality
Iruka should feel precise, fluid, and lightweight. The interface should evoke depth, quiet confidence, and readiness instead of hype. The emotional target is calm control under pressure: the product filters open data and surfaces only what matters.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use an operational theme inspired by sonar, depth charts, signal traces, and precise instrumentation. The visual system should be restrained in the product workspace and more atmospheric on marketing surfaces. Accent color should feel like muted copper or deep teal, not neon crypto orange. Avoid generic SaaS gradients, gamer glow, glassmorphism, playful rounded shapes, and purple-blue "AI" palettes.

### Design Principles
- Design the system first: tokens, surfaces, type, spacing, and interaction states should drive page work.
- Keep workspace pages sparse and operational: current state, one clear next action, low visual noise.
- Make Iruka memorable through atmosphere and structure, not gimmicks: washi texture, shoji-like lines, and instrument-like framing should be subtle and purposeful.
- Use calm hierarchy over loud emphasis: regular weight text, strong spacing, precise borders, and contrast should carry the interface.
- Remove one-off styling and repeated page-local patterns in favor of reusable primitives and shared utility classes.
